03

A practical agenda for governing complex technical systems.

How AI capacity becomes regulatory architecture.

My current research focuses on compute, export controls, democratic values, safety policy, and the institutional capacity required to govern powerful technical systems.

  • AI governance and policy evaluation
  • Compute governance and frontier AI regulation
  • Trust, safety, and democratic accountability

Where infrastructure, security, and regional politics collide.

Regional analysis matters because technology competition is not abstract. It moves through ports, cables, defense modernization, data rules, and alliance politics.

  • U.S.-China technology competition
  • South Asia and MENA risk analysis
  • Cybersecurity, 5G, and dual-use technology policy

Institutions fail before they admit they are failing.

The recurring question in my work is how governance bodies process technical uncertainty, capture, legitimacy, and coordination failure before crisis makes the weakness obvious.

  • Regulatory design and institutional capacity
  • Postcolonial analysis of law and power
  • Organizational incentives in technical standard-setting

Programs and tools that make complexity usable.

Beyond writing, I build workflows, visualizations, and field programs that make institutional problems more legible to teams that need to act.

  • D3.js and Leaflet prototypes for public-interest analysis
  • Local-first research and career-ops automation
  • Daadras education, relief, and digital-literacy programming
