about

A life organized around institutions, power, and public purpose.

A Washington, DC-based Pakistani writer and researcher shaped by SAIS, Lahore, and field institution-building.

01

Bio.

Institutions are not abstract to me; they are schools, cities, programs, and the habits they leave behind.

I completed an MA in International Relations at Johns Hopkins SAIS with a focus on Technology and Innovation Policy. My work connects export controls, cybersecurity, infrastructure resilience, and program design.

Before Washington, I grew up between Pakistani institutions that made education feel both fragile and necessary. That experience shaped the way I read power: through law, class, colonial inheritance, organizational incentives, and the lived consequences of institutional failure.

I do not treat research, writing, and building as separate identities. Research gives language, writing keeps the human cost visible, and prototypes test whether an idea can survive contact with use.
