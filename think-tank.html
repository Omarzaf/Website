02

The origin question.

Know the ideology, the expertise, and the money behind the institution before you trust the argument.

The first artifact was Think Tanks DC: a public interactive dataset mapping funding relationships between Washington policy institutions and donors, built from IRS 990 filings through ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer, USASpending.gov, OpenSecrets, and self-disclosed funding pages.

Its six panels made opacity visible: a funding heatmap, ideology-versus-funding chart, triple-funded institutions, transparency scatter plot, donor network graph, and rankings table. It did not prove capture. It made asymmetry inspectable.

03

How the RDL system works.

evidence boundary
Raw Data to canonical IDs

The active RDL repository treats Raw Data/ as the evidence boundary and resolves public exports for firms, clients, lobbyists, government entities, filings, contracts comparisons, issue mappings, text analysis, and revolving-door records.

source
data model
Canonical entity and edge universe

Stage 02 writes the canonical entity and edge universe. Later stages derive issue profiles, embeddings, graph metrics, communities, gap leads, and revolving-door vectors from that shared base rather than from disconnected tables.

model
gravity model
UMAP, Louvain, KDE, and gap score

UMAP gives the 2D issue space, Louvain finds graph communities, and spending-weighted KDE is compared against entity-count density. A high gap score means high money with a thinner organized community, not proof of capture.

04

What the animation is showing.

The animation is an explanation layer for the project, not a substitute for the full map.

The hero visual compresses two things: the completed Think Tanks DC transparency surface and the RDL issue-space graph. It shows how sources become records, records become canonical entities, and entities become searchable relationships with visible uncertainty.

Revolving-door arcs represent resolved career-transition records. Node size reflects observed spend or filing volume where available, while government nodes use target-side lobbying exposure rather than agency spending.

06

How to read it responsibly.

The release caveats are part of the product, not a footnote under it.

The map is exploratory and correlational. A high gap score means high money and lower organized community density in the modeled space; it does not prove influence, causality, intent, coordination, corruption, or wrongdoing.

Government target-side exposure is not government spending. Revolving-door records are transition records or events unless deduplication proves unique people. The 2025 slice remains partial unless refreshed source exports and validation reports say otherwise.
