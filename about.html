about

A life organized around institutions, power, and public purpose.

A Washington, DC-based Pakistani writer and researcher shaped by SAIS, Lahore, and field institution-building.

01

Bio.

I completed an MA in International Relations at Johns Hopkins SAIS after moving through political theory, human rights, development, and sustainability. That range gave me one durable question: how institutions decide whose lives become visible, protected, and worth planning around.

At SAIS and through Daadras, that question moved into technology and innovation policy: export controls, cybersecurity, infrastructure resilience, AI governance, and program design. Before Washington, I grew up between Pakistani institutions that made education feel both fragile and necessary, shaping the way I read power through law, class, colonial inheritance, organizational incentives, and the lived consequences of institutional failure.
