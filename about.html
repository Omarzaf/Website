about

A life organized around institutions, power, and public purpose.

Muhammad Umar Zafar is a Washington, DC-based Pakistani writer and policy researcher working where AI governance, geopolitical risk, and social-impact institutions overlap.

01

Bio.

I work where AI, geopolitics, and public-purpose institutions collide.

I completed an MA in International Relations at Johns Hopkins SAIS with a focus on Technology and Innovation Policy. My work connects AI governance, cybersecurity, infrastructure resilience, export controls, geopolitical risk, and program design.

Before Washington, I grew up between Pakistani institutions that made education feel both fragile and necessary. That experience shaped the way I read power: through law, class, colonial inheritance, organizational incentives, and the lived consequences of institutional failure.

I do not treat research, writing, and building as separate identities. The research gives language to systems, the writing keeps the human cost visible, and the prototypes test whether an idea can survive contact with use.
