project

Davis Projects for Peace.

A three-month Lahore intervention using theatre, role reversal, and facilitated dialogue to build a replicable peacebuilding model with Pashtun and Punjabi youth.

01

The method is rehearsal before persuasion.

The project treats theatre as civic infrastructure. Participants do not only discuss conflict; they inhabit each other's roles, test language in the room, and practice coexistence before it becomes an abstract demand.

02

Operating model.

Theatre and dialogue as a way to rehearse coexistence.

The Davis Projects for Peace grant supports a three-month intervention in Lahore with Pashtun and Punjabi youth. The project uses role reversal, theatre, and facilitated dialogue to build a replicable peacebuilding model.

The design moves from trust-building to story exchange, from story exchange to performance, and from performance to a practical guide that schools and community organizations can adapt.
